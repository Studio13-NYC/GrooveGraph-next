# Research workbench — minimum 10-question UX interview

Use with Cursor’s **question module** (or any survey tool): paste each **Question** and the **Options** as choices. Optional **Probe** lines are for short follow-ups in chat.

---

### 1. Primary job

**Question:** What is the main job you want the research workbench to do for you?

**Options:**
- Explore a topic and gather evidence quickly  
- Turn dialogue into a graph (triplets/claims) I can trust  
- Review and decide on many candidates in one sitting  
- Hand off structured findings to another tool or person  
- Something else  

**Probe:** If “something else,” what outcome in one sentence?

---

### 2. Session switching

**Question:** In a typical week, how often do you switch between multiple research sessions?

**Options:**
- Usually one session at a time  
- A few times per week  
- Several times per day  
- Rarely — one long-lived session  

---

### 3. Index (left column)

**Question:** For the session list (Index), what matters most?

**Options:**
- Scan titles and pick fast  
- See last updated / recency clearly  
- Create a new session with minimal friction  
- Search or filter when there are many sessions  
- Not sure yet  

---

### 4. Split layout (wide screens)

**Question:** For the draggable split between Investigation and Review, what default feels right?

**Options:**
- Closer to 50/50  
- More space for Investigation (~60–70%)  
- More space for Review (~60–70%)  
- Depends on task — I’ll drag it every time  
- I mostly use stacked / narrow layout  

---

### 5. Module color legend

**Question:** The colored “Module color coding” strip at the top — how do you feel about it?

**Options:**
- Helps me orient  
- Visual noise — I’d remove or shrink it  
- Only useful until I learn the colors  
- I didn’t notice it  

---

### 6. Triplet / graph review friction

**Question:** When working through relationship triplets, where is the biggest friction?

**Options:**
- Reading proposition + confidence + status  
- Editing entities, aliases, or verb  
- Deciding accept vs defer vs reject  
- Too many items — need batching or filters  
- No major friction  

---

### 7. Claims vs triplets

**Question:** How do you use **Claims** relative to **Graph review (triplets)**?

**Options:**
- Claims first — triplets are secondary  
- Triplets first — claims are secondary  
- I use both equally  
- I mostly ignore one of them  
- I’m not sure what the difference is  

**Probe:** What would make the distinction obvious?

---

### 8. Errors and trust

**Question:** When something fails (API error, empty state, stuck “busy”), what do you need most?

**Options:**
- Clear message + what to try next  
- Technical detail (request id, error code)  
- Retry without losing what I typed  
- I rarely hit errors  

---

### 9. Mobile / small screen

**Question:** On a phone or narrow window, what’s acceptable for investigation vs review?

**Options:**
- Stacked sections in one scroll — fine  
- Tabs to switch Investigation vs Review  
- I don’t use workbench on small screens  
- I need both columns visible — desktop only  

---

### 10. Success in one session

**Question:** At the end of a **good** session, what is true?

**Options:**
- I have sources I can cite  
- I have a graph I’m willing to export or share  
- I’ve decided most items (few “deferred”)  
- I saved time vs doing this in a generic chat  
- Other  

**Probe:** If “other,” what would you measure?

---

## Interview tips (short)

- **Order:** 1 → 3 → 2 establishes job, then concrete UI, then habits.  
- **Time:** ~12–15 minutes if you don’t probe every item.  
- **Closing:** “What one change would you ship first?” (free text in chat.)
